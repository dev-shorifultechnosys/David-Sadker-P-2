About

A teacher at heart — still learning out loud.

A warm, current biography for David Sadker’s writing, speaking, workshops, and forthcoming book updates.

Title: Author and Professor Emeritus · Workshop Leader Based in: Hawaii, USA Email: david@sadker.org

David Sadker is an author, Professor Emeritus, and workshop leader who has spent more than fifty years at the intersection of education, equity, and the everyday lives of students.

With his late wife Myra Sadker, David authored the landmark Failing at Fairness: How America's Schools Cheat Girls, a book that reshaped national conversation about gender bias in the classroom. The work continued in Still Failing at Fairness — co-authored with Karen Zittleman — and across hundreds of articles, talks, and television appearances.

As Professor Emeritus at American University in Washington, D.C., David trained generations of teachers, school leaders, and researchers. He has led workshops in nearly every U.S. state and abroad, helping educators see the quiet patterns that shape who gets to speak, who gets noticed, and who gets to thrive.

Today, writing from the Hawaiian coast, he continues that work through speaking, mentoring, and a forthcoming book that gathers the lessons of a lifetime in the classroom.
